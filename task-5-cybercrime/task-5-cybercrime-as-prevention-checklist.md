PART-A

**Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud**

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly increasing cybercrime in India, targeting users through deceptive payment requests and fake communication. In this crime, fraudsters trick victims into authorizing payments instead of receiving money.

Typically, the fraud begins when the victim receives a call, SMS, or message on apps like WhatsApp claiming to be from a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer. The scammer convinces the victim that they need to “receive” money and sends a UPI collect request. The victim, unaware, enters their UPI PIN, thinking it is required to receive funds. In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a payment, and money is transferred from the victim’s account to the fraudster.

College students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use digital payment apps and may lack awareness about such scams. Victims may lose significant amounts of money, face emotional stress, and in some cases, their personal information may also be misused.

The consequences include financial loss, compromised bank accounts, and reduced trust in digital payment systems. Reporting delays often make it difficult to recover lost funds.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful handling of UPI requests. Users must remember that receiving money never requires entering a UPI PIN.

PART-B

**Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)**

1. Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money—PIN is only required for sending payments.
2. Always verify UPI collect requests before approving them.
3. Do not share OTPs, PINs, or banking details with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank.
4. Avoid clicking on suspicious links received via SMS, email, or messaging apps.
5. Install apps only from official stores like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
6. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.
7. Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts.
8. Be cautious of “too good to be true” offers, job scams, or cashback schemes.
9. Regularly check your bank statements and transaction history.
10. Log out from public/shared devices after use.

**Financial/UPI Safety Tips:**

* Double-check the receiver’s UPI ID before sending money.
* Do not scan unknown QR codes—they may deduct money instead of adding it.

**Reporting Cybercrime:**

* Report incidents immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
* Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930**

Staying alert and informed is the best defense against cybercrime.
